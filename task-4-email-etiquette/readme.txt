In digital communication, poor email writing can often lead to misunderstandings and missed opportunities. For example, a student once sent an email to a professor without a proper subject line and used informal language like “Hey” and short, unclear sentences. As a result, the professor did not take the email seriously and ignored it, causing the student to miss an important deadline.

This situation highlights how improper digital communication can create real problems. It could have been avoided by using a clear subject line, a respectful greeting, and a well-structured message. Maintaining a professional tone and clarity is essential in academic and professional communication.

Through this task, I learned that effective communication is not just about sending a message, but about presenting it in a clear, respectful, and professional manner.
