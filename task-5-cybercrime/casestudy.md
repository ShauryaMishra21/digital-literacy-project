UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today. It involves tricking users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs or approving fraudulent payment requests.

A typical UPI fraud begins when a victim receives a phone call, SMS, or message from a scammer pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or a known contact. The scammer creates urgency, such as claiming that the victim’s bank account will be blocked or that a reward has been issued.

The victim is then asked to share an OTP or click on a malicious link, or they receive a “collect request” on their UPI app and are instructed to approve it. Once the victim approves the request or shares the OTP, money is instantly transferred from their account to the scammer.

Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may lack awareness about such scams. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and difficulty recovering the stolen money.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and cautious behavior while using digital payment systems.
