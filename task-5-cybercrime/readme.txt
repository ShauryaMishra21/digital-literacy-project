In this task, I explored cybercrime awareness by studying UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud, which is one of the most common types of online fraud in India. UPI fraud usually occurs when scammers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs or approving fraudulent payment requests.

A typical case involves a victim receiving a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or customer support agent. The scammer creates urgency by claiming that the victim’s account will be blocked or that they have received a reward. The victim is then asked to share an OTP, click on a suspicious link, or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app. Once approved, money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account. Students are often targeted due to lack of awareness, leading to financial loss and stress.

To prevent such cybercrimes, it is important to follow safe online practices. Users should never share their OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details with anyone. They should avoid approving unknown collect requests and always verify the receiver’s details before making any transaction. It is also important to avoid clicking on suspicious links, use strong passwords, and enable two-factor authentication. Apps should be downloaded only from trusted sources, and bank transactions should be monitored regularly. In case of fraud, it should be immediately reported on the National Cyber Crime Portal (https://cybercrime.gov.in) or by calling the helpline number 1930.

This task helped me understand the seriousness of cybercrime and the importance of being cautious while using digital payment systems.
